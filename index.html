Aquatic Products from 10 Japanese Prefectures Banned

The Japanese Government announced the plan to discharge the nuclear-contaminated water generated during the process of cooling the reactors at the Fukushima nuclear power station into the ocean after treatment from August 22, 2023.

In response to the plan, Hong Kong’s Centre for Food Safety has introduced stricter control measures for 10 prefectures. All live, frozen, chilled, dried or in any way preserved aquatic products, as well as sea salt and raw/processed seaweed is prohibited.

In addition, on-going control measures have been implemented specifically for Fukushima. The import of vegetables, fruits, milk, milk beverages and dried milk is banned. To import frozen or chilled game, meat, poultry, and poultry eggs, a radiation certificate is required.

For Ibaraki, Tochigi, Chiba and Gunma, the import of vegetables, fruits, milk, milk beverages and dried milk requires both an exporter certificate and a radiation certificate. For importing frozen or chilled game, meat, poultry and poultry eggs, a radiation certificate is necessary.

Chief Executive John Lee said Hong Kong has to be careful about the long-term risks of releasing nuclear-contaminated water into the sea.
